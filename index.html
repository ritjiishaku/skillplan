📚 Must-Read Books
The definitive reading list — recommended across Reddit, HN, and every senior AI engineer we know
🧠
AI Engineering
Chip Huyen · O'Reilly 2025
The #1 recommended book for AI engineers. Covers the full AI engineering stack: RAG, evaluation, agents, deployment, and monitoring. Chip taught ML at Stanford, worked at NVIDIA & Netflix.
🔧
The LLM Engineer's Handbook
Paul Iusztin & Maxime Labonne · 2024
End-to-end framework for building production LLM & RAG apps. Covers data collection, fine-tuning (LoRA/QLoRA), evaluation, and deployment. Highly practical — builds one complete system throughout.
🏗️
Designing ML Systems
Chip Huyen · O'Reilly 2022
The essential primer on ML system design under real-world constraints: data drift, retraining, reliability. Timeless principles that apply directly to LLM systems. Read before AI Engineering.
🔬
Build a Large Language Model from Scratch
Sebastian Raschka · Manning 2024
Build a GPT-like LLM in PyTorch from scratch. Best resource for understanding LLM internals: tokenization, attention, training loops. For engineers who want to understand, not just use, models.
✍️
Prompt Engineering for LLMs
Berryman & Ziegler · O'Reilly 2024
Written by core engineers who built GitHub Copilot. Systems-level thinking on prompting — the best book for understanding prompt engineering as an engineering discipline, not a collection of tricks.
🚀
Building LLMs for Production
Bouchard & Peters · Towards AI 2024
Practical guide to shipping LLMs: fine-tuning, deploying, scaling, maintaining. Packed with architecture examples and real deployment challenges for engineers aiming at LLM engineer roles.
